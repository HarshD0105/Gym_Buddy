Cardio is shorthand for cardiovascular training, and it encompasses any exercise—such as running, cycling, or dancing—that elevates your heart rate. Cardio is also considered aerobic exercise, meaning it demands elevated oxygen flow, which causes you to breathe harder

The upper body refers to your arms, forearms, shoulders, chest and hands. These muscles create a bridge from the chest, limbs and torso to your spinal column

The lower extremity refers to the part of the body from the hip to the toes. The lower extremity includes the hip, knee, and ankle joints, and the bones of the thigh, leg, and foot.

A device having an endless belt on which an individual walks or runs in place that is used for exercise and in tests of physiological functions see stress test.

The lat pulldown is an upper-body exercise that focuses on the muscles of your back. Much like the squat and bench press, it's considered a compound exercise — meaning that it targets many muscles at once.

A pec deck is a machine designed to increase strength and muscle mass in the chest. It's so effective that the American Council on Exercise ranked it as one of the best exercises for building chest muscles.

Leg curls target the hamstrings (biceps femoris, semitendinosus, and semimembranosus) and calf muscles (gastrocnemius and soleus muscles). When lowering your feet back down, your buttocks (gluteus muscles), thighs (quadriceps), and front of the shins (tibialis anterior) are activated too.

A seated cable row is a compound exercise that utilizes a weighted horizontal cable row machine to work muscle groups in your back and arms. Cable machines include a bench for comfortable seating and foot plates to brace yourself against as you pull the weighted cable.
